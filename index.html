A suggested path

work top to bottom, or jump around

The phases below follow the order most learners need things: solid kana first, then attaching sounds to kanji (the most common sticking point for read-first learners), then speaking, then immersion — learning directly from native media — then testing. It's a hub, though — every page stands alone.

Phase 1 · Foundations

Lock in the basics

Drill the kana sheets until recall is instant, and keep the particle guide handy — particles are what make sentences parse.

Phase 2 · Readings

Give kanji a sound

On the kanji page, learn on'yomi vs kun'yomi, then tap 🔊 to hear each reading. Drill with the reading quiz until 水 instantly "sounds like" みず.

Phase 3 · Output

Start producing sound

Use the speaking page: shadow the audio of high-frequency phrases out loud. A couple of minutes a day builds the skill silent reading never touches.

Phase 4 · Immersion

Learn from native media

Watch and read real Japanese, and mine it: asbplayer + Yomitan turn a subtitle line into an Anki card in two keystrokes; mokuro does the same for manga. The Setup page wires it all up — every mined card feeds back into the hub on the next sync.

Phase 5 · Prove it

Test & track

Run the dynamic quizzes — they regenerate every time. With Anki connected, the My vocab mode quizzes the exact cards in the deck.
